EPISTEMOLOGICAL_QUESTIONS

The following paragraphs pertain to the author of this website asking itself questions about the nature of its own subjective notions of reality (especially while considering the possibility that infinitely many phenomenally distinct and non-overlapping universes exist simultaneously inside of exactly one all-encompassing MULTIVERSE).

One assumption (which is an extension of the pansychism hypothesis) about reality is that reality contains every imaginable phenomenon (and every imaginable permutation of phenomena) and that reality itself is one all-encompassing mind which, from the most macroscopic level possible, makes no distinction between a phenomenon and its respective noumenon because every phenomenon exists immutably and timelessly. (A noumenon is the absence of some phenomenon from the perspective of some partial frame of reference. By contrast, all phenomena are present simultaneously inside of an omniscient frame of reference).

Why am I experiencing some phenomena but not experiencing any other phenomena?

Are there any phenomena occurring outside of this frame of reference?

If and when I die, will my apparently linear space-time trajectory (i.e. life time) cease to exist or will it be extended to include some future life time?

If such continuity of consciousness is possible, how many discrete life times does exactly one linear space-time trajectory (as rendered by exactly one partial frame of reference) pass through?

How many times does the same partial frame of reference render the same unique permutation of phenomena which a single life time represents?

How many times does any partial frame of reference render the same unique permutation of phenomena which a single life time represents?
